Mark Zuckerberg

About

Mark Zuckerberg is an American computer programmer
and Internet entrepreneur. He is a co-founder and the
chief executive officer of Facebook, one of the most
popular social networking websites in the world.
Zuckerberg is also the co-founder of the Chan Zuckerberg
Initiative, a philanthropic organization that focuses
on education, health, and science.

In 2012, Zuckerberg married Priscilla Chan, a
fellow Harvard student. The couple has two daughters.

Biography

Zuckerberg was born in White Plains, New York, in 1984. He
attended Harvard University, where he studied computer science
and philosophy. In 2004, while still a student at Harvard, Zuckerberg
co-founded Facebook with Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum,
Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. The website quickly gained popularity,
and by 2006 it had over 100 million users. In 2012, Facebook
went public and Zuckerberg became a billionaire.

Early life and education :--

->>Zuckerberg attended Ardsley High School in Ardsley, New York,
before transferring to Phillips Exeter Academy, a

Co-founding Facebook:--

->>In 2003, Zuckerberg enrolled at Harvard University,
where he studied computer science and philosophy. While at Harvard,
Zuckerberg co-founded Facebook with Eduardo Saverin, Andrew
McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. The website was
originally called "Thefacebook" and was designed to allow Harvard
students to connect with each other. Thefacebook quickly gained popularity
and by 2004 it had over 1 million users.

->>In 2005, Zuckerberg dropped out of Harvard to focus on Facebook.
The company moved its headquarters to Palo Alto, California, and Zuckerberg
became its CEO. Under Zuckerberg's leadership, Facebook has grown
into one of the most popular social networking websites in the world.
It has over 2 billion active users and is used by people of all ages,
from all over the world.

Philanthropy

->>In 2015, Zuckerberg and his wife, Priscilla Chan, co-founded
the Chan Zuckerberg Initiative, a philanthropic organization that
focuses on education, health, and science. The organization has pledged
to donate $99 billion over the next 50 years.
